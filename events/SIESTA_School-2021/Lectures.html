SIESTA School 2021: Lectures

In this page you will find the list of lectures of the school. It is a combination of new recordings uploaded to the SIESTA Project YouTube channel and a few fragments of a recent webinar given in the context of the MaX Centre of Excellence.

On Monday 21st June we released the first seven lectures, with a total duration of 2h:

  • If you are new to SIESTA we strongly recommend that you watch all these lectures, in the suggested order. You will need a reasonable understanding of these lectures in order to follow the tutorials next week.
  • If you are an experienced SIESTA user, you may also find valuable, up to date information in this first batch of lectures.

On Thursday 24rd June we released lectures 8 to 12, with a total duration of 1h15min. These lectures introduce a range of postprocessing tools, as well as some intermediate/advanced functionalities that may be of interest to all the students.

List of lectures:

  1. Introduction to SIESTA [slides, video (19 mins)], by Emilio Artacho (Nanogune, Ikerbasque, DIPC and University of Cambridge). Please watch the first 19 minutes of this recent webinar.
  2. Some internals of the SIESTA method (part 1) [slides, video (18 mins)], by Jose M. Soler (Universidad Autónoma de Madrid).
  3. Some internals of the SIESTA method (part 2) [slides, video (19 mins)], by Jose M. Soler (Universidad Autónoma de Madrid).
  4. The pseudopotential concept [slides, video (16 mins)], by Alberto García (ICMAB-CSIC).
  5. Basis sets for SIESTA: I. Non-orthogonal representations and LCAO [slides, video (14 mins)], by Emilio Artacho (Nanogune, Ikerbasque, DIPC and University of Cambridge).
  6. Basis sets for SIESTA: II. Finite support numerical bases used in SIESTA [slides, video (18 mins)], by Emilio Artacho (Nanogune, Ikerbasque, DIPC and University of Cambridge).
  7. Electronic-Structure solvers in SIESTA: features and performance [slides, video (16 mins)], by Alberto García (ICMAB-CSIC). Please watch the second talk of this recent webinar.
  8. SIESTA postprocessing: an overview [slides, video (16 mins)], by Pol Febrer (ICN2).
  9. Post-processing tools for SIESTA crystal structure, vibrations and grid properties [slides, video (19 mins)], by Andrei Postnikov (Université de Lorraine).
  10. Post-processing tools for analysis of lattice dynamics following SIESTA+Vibra calculation [slides, video (16 mins)], by Andrei Postnikov (Université de Lorraine).
  11. Spin-Orbit Coupling [slides, video (10 mins)], by Ramón Cuadrado (University of Southampton).
  12. Introduction to Wannierization [slides, video (16 mins)], by Javier Junquera (Universidad de Cantabria).

More lectures will be released during the week - stay tuned!
